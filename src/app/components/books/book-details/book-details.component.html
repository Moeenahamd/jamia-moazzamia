Description:

"1984" by George Orwell is a dystopian novel published in 1949. The story is set in a future society ruled by a totalitarian Party, led by Big Brother. Winston Smith, a Party member, rebels against the oppressive regime, navigating a world of surveillance, propaganda, and thought control. His forbidden relationship with Julia adds another layer of danger to his defiance. As they strive for individual freedom, they face betrayal and manipulation. The novel delves into themes of authoritarianism , propaganda, manipulation, and the consequences of constant surveillance. It serves as a stark warning about unchecked government control and the loss of personal liberties. "1984" remains a captivating and influential work, critiquing oppressive political systems and highlighting the importance of individual freedom.
